LEAGUES

GZD Badminton Club competes in the Manchester Badminton League, going up against clubs across the city in properly competitive match play. All our sessions, casual or competitive, are played with goose feather shuttlecocks, so you are always training at match standard.

01 League Play

Our league team represents GZD in the Manchester Badminton League throughout the season. League matches are a step up from club nights: faster, more tactical, and played to win. If you are keen to get involved, speak to the organiser directly and we will assess where you would fit best.

Players who feature in more than 2 league fixtures per season are required to hold a full club membership.

02 Play Grades

Not sure where you sit on the competitive scale? We use a simple internal grading system via eBadders to help match players to the right level, whether that is casual club nights or league selection. The guide below breaks down each grade so you know what to expect and where you stand.

Play Grades Guide (PDF)
E/D-

Beginner

New to badminton or returning after a long break. Still building grip, footwork, and getting the shuttle over the net consistently.

D+

Improver

Grasp of the basics, sustaining a rally with some regularity, becoming aware of positioning and starting to think about shot placement.

C-

Lower Intermediate

Growing confidence, core shots becoming more reliable. Holding your own in casual competitive play and building rallies with intention.

C+

Intermediate

Solid, well-rounded club player. Footwork gets you to most shots, shot selection is purposeful, comfortable in singles and doubles.

B-

Higher Intermediate

Strong club player with a competitive edge. Efficient movement, wide shot variety, clear tactical awareness. Approaching or in league-level play.

B+/A

Advanced

Experienced, high-level player. Technically refined, tactically intelligent, physically disciplined. Consistent performer in GZD's competitive squad.

A+

County Level

Competes or has competed at county level or equivalent. Technically precise, tactically superior, mentally composed in high-stakes situations.

Grades are a rough guide, not a rigid ranking. Speak to the organiser if you feel you have progressed.
